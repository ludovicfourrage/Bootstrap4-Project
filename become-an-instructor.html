LIFE OF A NUCAMP INSTRUCTOR
NuCamp instructors are all about student's success. They spend 6-8 hours helping their students every week: 3-4 hours online during the week and 3-4 hours during the in-person workshop.

During the week: They set the tempo of the class. They give instructions, respond to student's IMs and messages online, answer questions and make sure no one is feeling stuck. They check-in with students who may fall behind. They engage the classroom online, share best-practices and prepare them for the end of week workshop. They help students with their personal projects, do research with them. They're always available to help.

At the end of the week: They animate the in-person workshops. They make sure the students are grouped correctly, and help them with the workshop assignment. They explain the common pitfalls. They typically spend more time with students who are having a difficult time. They review each student's assignment and offer personalized feedback.

Throughout the course/curriculum: They help students prepare for their job search. Using the NuCamp JobSearch Playbook they guide students on how to update their LinkedIn profile, prepare for job interviews, and apply to job postings.

"NuCamp delivers the right balance between online learning and immersive bootcamps, at the right price."

What started as a goal became reality for our students.
Read about their experiences below.

Daniil Krets:
"I really appreciated NuCamp's approach to teaching: it's perfect for people working 40hrs a week and having no opportunity to give up their jobs to learn to code. The instructor helps deal with concept that are hard to understand, share best practices and give opportunity to discuss assignments and learn online as well as in person. In comparison one day bootcamps or full-time 12-week courses are expensive and time-consuming.  NuCamp's approach is flexible, goes deeper and the price is very affordable."
Kyle Getty:
“I bought a lot of online courses, from Udemy, codeplace.com etc… and the most challenging aspect is there’s no-one to help when you get stuck. Posting a message to their forums is a barrier, and you never know if or when someone is going to provide a helpful answer. I learned HTML, CSS and Javascript with NuCamp and enjoyed having direct access to the instructor. Being able to attend office hours and meet with the other students during in-person workshops meant I never left stuck or unable to move forward.”
Brandon K. Richard:
​"Before joining NuCamp I took part in another course that implemented a mentor system for each project. While this worked out okay, NuCamp's system of in-person group sessions was more helpful and allowed for better, more personal feedback."
Andrii Tymoshenko:
“I especially like that we met in person every week for workshops and got the opportunity to collaborate with other students. It’s much harder to meet and collaborate online with a group, and never as efficient as in person interactions.”
Jenn Cai: "With the countless MOOCs available these days, I have yet to commit to one and complete it. I found the learning experience offered by NuCamp very effective in helping me reach my goals. It provided structure and collaboration that are otherwise missing when following online material on your own. Overall, it was an enjoyable experience and would be a great resource for serious learners."
